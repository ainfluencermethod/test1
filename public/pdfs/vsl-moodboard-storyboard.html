Usage Rules

  • Green (#6bfb82) — ONLY for CTA buttons, "BREZPLAČNO" text, and € money figures. Never decorative.
  • Purple glow (#b388ff) — For highlights, underlines, emphasis text. Can be used as rim light color.
  • White — All headings, main body text overlays. Always bold sans-serif (Inter, Montserrat, or similar).
  • Deep backgrounds — All lower thirds, text backgrounds use #0a0c1e at 85% opacity.

💡 Lighting & Camera Setup

Lighting Design

  • Key light: Single softbox at 45° camera-left, slightly above eye level. Soft, controlled — creates dimension without harsh shadows.
  • Rim/hair light: LED strip or tube light at camera-right-rear, gelled deep purple (#7c3aed). Creates a purple edge on shoulder/head that separates subject from background.
  • Background: Completely dark or very minimal. If any set dressing, light it with dim purple LED strips. Aim for near-black with subtle purple gradients.
  • Fill: Minimal to none. Let the shadows be deep. Crushed blacks are part of the look.
  • Overall ratio: High contrast, 4:1 or 5:1 key-to-fill ratio. Moody, cinematic.

Camera Settings

  • Lens: 35mm f/1.8 (or 50mm f/1.4 on crop sensor)
  • Aperture: f/1.8–f/2.0 for shallow depth of field
  • ISO: Keep as low as possible (100–400)
  • Frame rate: 24fps for cinematic feel
  • Resolution: 4K (downscale to 1080p in post for extra sharpness)
  • Color profile: S-Log3 or V-Log if available. Otherwise, flattest picture profile available.
  • White balance: Manual 5600K for key light. Let the purple rim stay purple naturally.
  • Framing: Rule of thirds — eyes at upper third. Slight headroom. Subject offset to one side to leave space for text overlays on the opposite side.

🎥 Visual Style Guide

Talking Head Shots

Two setups needed — Medium shot (chest up) for most dialogue, and Close-up (face/shoulders) for intense moments, hooks, and CTAs. Both with shallow DOF, dark background, purple rim light. Subject looks directly into camera (no teleprompter look — natural, eye-to-eye connection).

B-Roll Style

  • Screen recordings: Clean, high-res captures of dashboards, analytics, social media profiles. Apply a subtle purple tint to the screen edges. Use smooth mouse movements — never jerky.
  • Screenshots reveals: Student results appear with a smooth scale-up + slight bounce animation. Add a subtle glow/shadow behind each screenshot.
  • AI tools montage: Quick cuts (0.5–1s each) of various AI interfaces — ChatGPT, Midjourney, analytics dashboards. All slightly desaturated with purple overlay.
  • Lifestyle B-roll: If available — laptop on desk, phone notifications, modern workspace. All dark-lit with purple accent lighting.

Kinetic Typography

  • Font: Inter Black or Montserrat ExtraBold. Always uppercase for impact text.
  • Animation: Text slams in from below with a slight overshoot bounce. Hold 2–3 seconds. Fade or scale-out.
  • Positioning: Lower third for subtitles/emphasis. Center screen for standalone text slides. Numbers (€1,840, 12,300) get extra size + green color (#6bfb82).
  • Key numbers always animated: Counter/odometer effect for monetary figures (€0 → €1,840 rapid count).
  • Drop shadow: Every text overlay gets a subtle black shadow (2px offset, 8px blur) for readability against any background.

Transitions

  • Standard cut: Sharp cut on dialogue. Most common transition (70% of cuts).
  • Smooth zoom: 1.05x scale punch-in between takes of the same shot (to hide jump cuts). Very common in modern YouTube VSLs.
  • Whip pan: Fast horizontal wipe between major sections (Hook → Opportunity, Proof → Event, etc.).
  • Glitch effect: Short (3–5 frame) RGB split + horizontal offset glitch for emphasis moments (big numbers, bold claims).
  • Fade to black: Only between major sections. 0.5s fade. Never within sections.

Color Grade

  • Overall: Desaturated by 15–20%. Crushed blacks (lift shadows slightly then crush).
  • Split toning: Purple (#4c1d95) in shadows, teal (#0d9488) in highlights. Subtle — don't overdo.
  • Skin tones: Protect skin tones from the color grade. Use a qualified secondary to keep natural warmth.
  • Contrast: High contrast overall. S-curve in Lumetri/DaVinci — push whites, crush blacks.
  • Vignette: Subtle corner vignette (-0.3 to -0.5) to draw eye to center.

🎯 Reference Creators & Style Notes

Iman Gadzhi's VSLs

Study for: Clean talking head framing, dramatic single-source lighting, confidence in delivery, premium feel. His "Monetize" VSL is the gold standard structure. Note how the background is almost completely black with minimal set design — all focus on the speaker.

Alex Hormozi

Study for: Bold kinetic typography overlays, rapid cuts between sentences (no dead air), screenshot proof-stacking technique. His editing pace is relentless — every 2-3 seconds has a visual change (zoom, text, cut). Adapt this energy for the proof stack section.

Modern Course VSLs

Study for: Student result reveals (screenshot slides in, zooms to key metric), event date countdown graphics, CTA button animations, lower-third name/result bars. The "social proof wall" technique — multiple results shown rapidly in sequence.

🔊 Audio & Sound Design

Background Music

  • Style: Dark, electronic ambient. Think Hans Zimmer "Time" meets tech startup trailer. Deep bass, subtle synth pads, building tension.
  • Volume: -18dB to -22dB under dialogue. Never compete with voice.
  • Dynamics: Start minimal in Hook, build through Opportunity, peak during Proof Stack transitions, drop for Event explanation, rise again for CTA.
  • Track change: Subtle shift at each major section. Don't use the same loop throughout.
  • Search terms: "dark cinematic ambient" "tech trailer music" "epic corporate dark"

Sound Effects

  • Whoosh: On every whip pan transition and text slam-in. Short, punchy.
  • UI click/pop: Subtle digital sound when text appears or screenshots reveal. Think iOS notification but softer.
  • Bass drop: Sub-bass hit (80Hz, 0.3s) on big number reveals (€41,127, 200M+).
  • Cash register / "cha-ching": Subtle, not cheesy. On first money mention only (€1,840).
  • Tension riser: Reverse cymbal or synth swell leading into each new section.
  • Glitch sound: Digital artifacts sound paired with glitch visual transition.

Voice Recording

  • Microphone: Condenser or dynamic broadcast mic (SM7B, Rode NT1). Close placement for intimate feel.
  • Room: Dead room, no reverb. The voice should sound dry, present, and close.
  • Processing: Light compression (3:1), de-ess, high-pass at 80Hz. No heavy EQ — keep it natural.
  • Delivery: Pici speaks confident, conversational Slovenian. Not shouty. Not monotone. Varies pace — slower for emphasis, faster when stacking proof. Pauses before big reveals.

✂️ Editing Pace Guide

The editing rhythm should mirror the emotional arc. Faster cuts = more energy. Slower = more weight.

Opportunity (0:15–1:00)12–16 CPM4–5sModerate. Mix of B-roll montage (fast) and talking head (slower).

🎨 Color Grading Specs

Primary Correction

  • Exposure: Slightly underexpose (-0.3 to -0.5 stops) for dark, moody feel
  • Contrast: +25 to +35 (high contrast)
  • Highlights: -15 (retain detail)
  • Shadows: -20 to -30 (crush blacks)
  • Blacks: -10 to -15 (deep crush)
  • Whites: +10 (keep some pop)
  • Saturation: -15 to -20 (desaturated look)
  • Vibrance: -5 to -10

Split Toning

  • Shadow hue: 270° (purple)
  • Shadow saturation: 15–20%
  • Highlight hue: 180° (teal/cyan)
  • Highlight saturation: 8–12%
  • Balance: -15 (favor shadows)

Secondary Corrections

  • Skin tones: Use HSL qualifier to isolate skin, boost saturation +10, add slight warmth (+5 orange). Protect from overall desaturation.
  • Vignette: -0.4 amount, 50 midpoint, 60 roundness

LUT Reference: If using a LUT as starting point, try "Film Noir" or "Teal & Orange" variants, then shift the orange toward purple in shadows. Commercial LUT packs: Tropic Colour "Dark Moody," Peter McKinnon "Dark & Moody."

🔤 Text Animation Style Guide

Standard Text Slam

  • Duration: 0.3s ease-out
  • Motion: Scale from 120% → 100% + position from 30px below → 0
  • Overshoot: Slight bounce (elastic ease, 1 bounce)
  • Hold: 2–3 seconds
  • Exit: Scale down to 95% + fade (0.2s)

Counter/Odometer Animation

  • Duration: 1.0–1.5s
  • Easing: Ease-out (fast start, slow end)
  • Start: €0 or 0
  • End: Target number
  • Add subtle tick/click SFX during count
  • Final number gets a scale pulse (102% → 100%)

Lower-Third Name Cards

  • Background: Semi-transparent dark bar (#0a0c1e at 85%)
  • Height: 60px
  • Entry: Slide in from left, 0.4s ease-out
  • Purple accent line (4px) on top of the bar
  • Text: Name (bold white) · Details (regular, #B8B8B8)
  • Hold: Full duration of the testimonial
  • Exit: Slide out left, 0.3s

Glitch Effect Specs

  • Duration: 3–5 frames (0.12–0.2s at 24fps)
  • RGB split: Offset Red +8px right, Blue +8px left
  • Horizontal slice displacement: 3–5 random horizontal slices offset 5–15px
  • Brightness flash: +30% for 1 frame
  • Pair with digital glitch SFX
  • Use sparingly: max 4–5 times per VSL

📱 Vertical Cut (Reels/TikTok) — Special Notes

The vertical cut is NOT the full VSL. It's a condensed version optimized for mobile-first short-form platforms.

  • Duration: 60–90 seconds max
  • Structure: Hook (0:00–0:10) → 2–3 best testimonials (0:10–0:40) → Event + CTA (0:40–1:00)
  • Skip: Opportunity section, Authority section, detailed event breakdown — no room
  • Text overlays: Must be larger (30% bigger than landscape version) for mobile readability
  • Captions: Add burned-in Slovenian captions (auto-caption or manual). 80% of mobile viewers watch without sound initially.
  • CTA: End with "Link v bio" instead of "Klikni spodaj" for IG. Show URL briefly but rely on bio link.
  • Aspect ratio conversion: Crop from 16:9 master, reframe subject to center. May need manual keyframing for medium shots where subject is off-center.
